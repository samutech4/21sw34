About Aurangzeb

Aurangzeb, also known as Alamgir, was the sixth Mughal emperor who ruled over a large part of the Indian subcontinent from 1658 to 1707. He is often remembered for his extensive military campaigns, administrative reforms, and for expanding the Mughal Empire to its greatest territorial extent.
